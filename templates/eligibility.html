Eligibility

Am I Eligible?

  • Age: Donors must be at least 16 to 18 years old, depending on local regulations.
  • Weight: Donors must weight at least 50 kilograms.
  • Health: Donors must be in good health, free from acute illnesses or infections.
  • Hemoglobin Levels: Donors need adequate hemoglobin levels to prevent anemia.
  • Travel History: Certain travel history may affect eligibility, especially in areas with diseases like malaria.
  • Medical History: Donors are asked about past surgeries, medications, and medical conditions.
  • Lifestyle Factors: High-risk behaviors like intravenous drug use or unprotected sex may defer individuals from donating.
  • Pregnancy and Breastfeeding: Pregnant or breastfeeding individuals are usually not eligible to donate blood.
  • Piercings and Tattoos: Depending on local regulations, there may be deferral periods after getting a piercing or tattoo.
  • Vaccinations: Some vaccines may require deferral periods before donating blood.
  • Recent Blood Donation: Donors must wait at least eight weeks (56 days) between donations of whole blood.
